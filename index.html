What is a Croissant?

Croissant

/k(r)wäˈsänt,k(r)wäˈsäN/

"a buttery, flaky, viennoiserie pastry of Austrian origin, but mostly associated with France. Croissants are named for their historical crescent shape and, like other viennoiserie, are made of a layered yeast-leavened dough."

Where to buy a Croissant?


Croissants are available at any nearby grocery store. Particularly in Florida they are available at Publix, Walmart, Winn-Dixie, Whole Foods, Traders Joes and more! You can also find them at any local bakery or special request that they be made!

How to make a Croissant?

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 teaspoons active dry yeast*
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 1/4 cups unsalted butter, cold (2 1/2 sticks)
  • 1 cup milk (you may need slightly more or less)
  • egg wash (1 large egg beaten with a teaspoon or two of water)
